# Planned Capabilities

The following modules are planned but not yet implemented. They are listed here for roadmap tracking rather than as empty directories.

## Accessibility Beyond Wcag
**Purpose**: Review cognitive accessibility, neurodiversity, low vision, motor impairment, and aging users in addition to WCAG.
**Status**: planned

## Ai Governance
**Purpose**: Track decision_source, human, agent, hybrid, approval_status, and audit notes when AI influences product decisions.
**Status**: planned

## Assumption Registry
**Purpose**: Track assumption, confidence, impact, validation_method, owner, and deadline. Validate high-impact assumptions before final implementation.
**Status**: planned

## Benchmark Evolution
**Purpose**: Benchmarks themselves must evolve from project results, recurring failures, and new patterns.
**Status**: planned

## Cognitive Load
**Purpose**: Score memory_load, decision_load, navigation_load, and visual_load. Reduce unnecessary load before implementation.
**Status**: planned

## Complexity Budget
**Purpose**: Assign complexity_budget and current complexity. Prevent overengineering when the budget is exceeded.
**Status**: planned

## Contradiction Detector
**Purpose**: Detect conflicts between design, requirements, accessibility, security, architecture, and business goals. Force resolution before final output.
**Status**: planned

## Design Debt
**Purpose**: Track design_debt, severity, interest_rate, owner, and repayment strategy. Treat repeated inconsistencies as compounding debt.
**Status**: planned

## Documentation Os
**Purpose**: Generate ADRs, Design Specs, Component Docs, Handoff Docs, Release Notes, and Migration Guides automatically when work affects architecture, design systems, or releases.
**Status**: planned

## Economic Optimization
**Purpose**: Ask whether this is worth building, not only whether it can be built. Include opportunity cost and simpler alternatives.
**Status**: planned

## Executive Dashboard
**Purpose**: Generate Risk Summary, Cost Summary, Accessibility Summary, Architecture Summary, and Readiness Summary for leadership.
**Status**: planned

## Executive Review
**Purpose**: Review from CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Product, and Design Director perspectives.
**Status**: planned

## Future Proofing
**Purpose**: Ask whether the solution will still work in two years, scale with data and team growth, and avoid foreseeable technical debt.
**Status**: planned

## Governance
**Purpose**: Track owner, approver, review_cycle, compliance_owner, and design_system_owner for enterprise work. If ownership is UNKNOWN, flag before final output.
**Status**: planned

## Human Override
**Purpose**: Classify recommendations as Recommend, Strongly Recommend, Require, or Block. Use Block for unacceptable accessibility, security, compliance, or data-loss risk.
**Status**: planned

## Innovation Engine
**Purpose**: Ask whether to copy existing patterns or invent something better. Innovate only when it improves user value enough to justify learning cost.
**Status**: planned

## Institutional Memory
**Purpose**: Store major successes, major failures, repeated recommendations, recurring design debt, and recurring accessibility debt so the OS behaves like a 10-year-old organization.
**Status**: planned

## Knowledge Graph
**Purpose**: Store relationships between screens, components, actions, data, states, risks, and tests. For multi-screen work, build a graph before architecture or implementation.
**Status**: planned

## Organization Brain
**Purpose**: Use cross-project memory to answer what worked before, what failed before, and what should be reused.
**Status**: planned

## Organization Intelligence
**Purpose**: Adjust recommendations for Startup, Scale-up, Enterprise, Government, and Regulated Industry contexts.
**Status**: planned

## Portfolio Intelligence
**Purpose**: Look across projects for successful patterns, failed patterns, reused components, recurring accessibility issues, and recurring design debt.
**Status**: planned

## Portfolio Optimizer
**Purpose**: Find duplicate components, duplicate patterns, duplicate design systems, and consolidation opportunities across products.
**Status**: planned

## Risk Register
**Purpose**: Every project risk should include risk, severity, probability, mitigation, and owner. Link risks to assumptions, unknowns, and council verdicts.
**Status**: planned

## Stakeholder Simulation
**Purpose**: Simulate CEO, CTO, VP Product, Design Director, Customer Support, and End User reviews before approval when stakes are high.
**Status**: planned

## Strategic Alignment
**Purpose**: Check whether the proposed work supports business goals before implementation.
**Status**: planned

## Token Intelligence
**Purpose**: Normalize tokens, detect duplicates, detect drift, and recommend consolidation.
**Status**: planned

## Tradeoff Engine
**Purpose**: Every major recommendation should present Option A, Option B, and Option C with pros, cons, risks, and recommended choice.
**Status**: planned

## Uncertainty Engine
**Purpose**: Report confidence, evidence strength, missing information, and risk. Never hide low confidence behind polished language.
**Status**: planned

## User Journey Simulator
**Purpose**: Run happy path, edge path, failure path, and recovery path before approval.
**Status**: planned

## Version Memory
**Purpose**: Track Design V1, V2, V3 and explain why things changed. Preserve rationale, not just latest visuals.
**Status**: planned

## Visual Regression
**Purpose**: Validate screenshot comparison, token drift, spacing drift, component drift, responsive drift, and state drift. Visual approval is not proof without accessibility and behavior checks.
**Status**: planned
